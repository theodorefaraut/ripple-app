FAQ

Why do I need to authorize access to Apple Music?

Ripple needs to access your library to display your playlists, recommendations, and allow you to add tracks to your favorites or playlists.

What's the difference between the two modes?

Favorites mode: swipe right adds the track to your Apple Music favorites.
Add to Playlist mode: swipe right adds the track to a playlist of your choice.

How do I change the swipe mode?

Tap the hand icon (üñêÔ∏è) at the top of the screen. You can choose between Favorites mode or Add to Playlist mode, and select the target playlist.

How do I add a track to a specific playlist?

From the track list, tap the + icon next to the heart. You can choose an existing playlist or create a new one.

What is the "For You" section?

This section displays playlists recommended by Apple Music based on your music tastes. Perfect for discovering new tracks!

Why are some tracks grayed out?

Grayed out tracks are those you've already processed (swiped). They remain visible for reference and you can still interact with them.

My created playlists don't appear

Recently created playlists may take a few minutes to appear (Apple Music limitation). Pull down to refresh.

Does Ripple delete my tracks?

No, never. Ripple never deletes any tracks from your library. It simply helps you organize your music faster.

Does the app require an Apple Music subscription?

Yes, an active Apple Music subscription is required to use Ripple.

How do I delete my data?

All data is stored locally on your device. Simply delete the app to erase all data.

Is the app free?

Yes, Ripple is completely free and ad-free.
